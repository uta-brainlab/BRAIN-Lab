About Our Lab

Understanding the brain through computational approaches

Our Mission

The Brain and Artificial Intelligence Research Laboratory (BRAINAI) at the University of Texas at Arlington is dedicated to advancing our understanding of brain function and mental health through the innovative application of computational methods and artificial intelligence.

Led by Dr. Xi Zhu, our interdisciplinary team combines expertise in neuroscience, psychiatry, computer science, and engineering to develop novel approaches for analyzing complex neural data and understanding the mechanisms underlying cognitive processes and psychiatric disorders.

Through our research, we aim to bridge the gap between computational theory and clinical application, ultimately contributing to improved diagnostic tools and therapeutic interventions for mental health conditions.

Research Focus

Computational Psychiatry

Developing computational models to better understand the neural basis of psychiatric disorders, with a particular focus on psychosis, schizophrenia, and mood disorders.

Neural Circuit Modeling

Investigating how neural circuits give rise to cognitive functions and how disruptions in these circuits contribute to psychiatric symptoms.

Neuroimaging Analysis

Creating advanced methods for analyzing fMRI, EEG, and other neuroimaging data to extract meaningful patterns and biomarkers of brain function and dysfunction.

Machine Learning in Psychiatry

Applying machine learning and deep learning approaches to identify novel patterns in clinical and neuroimaging data that can inform diagnosis and treatment.

Our Approach

Interdisciplinary Collaboration

We believe that groundbreaking research emerges at the intersection of disciplines. Our lab brings together researchers from neuroscience, computer science, psychology, psychiatry, and biomedical engineering to tackle complex questions about the brain and mental health.

Translational Focus

While we engage in fundamental computational research, we maintain a strong focus on translating our findings into tools and insights that can ultimately improve clinical practice and patient outcomes.

Open Science

We are committed to the principles of open science, making our methods, data, and software tools available to the broader scientific community whenever possible to accelerate discovery and innovation.

Funding & Support

Our research is supported by funding from various sources, including:

  • National Institutes of Health (NIH)
  • National Science Foundation (NSF)
  • Brain & Behavior Research Foundation
  • University of Texas at Arlington

We are grateful for the support that enables our research and the advancement of knowledge in computational neuroscience and psychiatry.
