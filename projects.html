Research Projects

Cutting-edge research at the intersection of neuroscience and artificial intelligence

Current Research Initiatives

At BRAINAI@UTA, our research spans multiple domains at the intersection of computational methods, neuroscience, and psychiatry. We employ cutting-edge techniques from machine learning and artificial intelligence to better understand brain function and mental health conditions.

Explore our ongoing projects below to learn more about our research directions and how they contribute to advancing our understanding of the brain and psychiatric disorders.

ABCD Study: ACE and BMI Association

This project investigates the association between Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs) and Body Mass Index (BMI) in children and adolescents using data from the Adolescent Brain Cognitive Development (ABCD) study.

Key Research Questions:

  • How do early life adversities influence weight trajectory in developing brains?
  • What neural mechanisms mediate the relationship between stress and weight regulation?
  • Can we identify protective factors that promote resilience against adverse health outcomes?
Learn More Project Brief

PTSD Biotypes

This project aims to identify distinct biological subtypes (biotypes) of Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) using machine learning approaches applied to multimodal neuroimaging and clinical data.

Key Research Goals:

  • Develop unsupervised learning methods to identify clusters of PTSD patients with similar neural patterns
  • Investigate how neurobiological subtypes relate to symptom profiles and treatment outcomes
  • Create predictive models to guide personalized treatment selection based on biotype
Learn More Project Brief

Trait-Like vs. State-Like Biomarkers

This project investigates the distinction between trait-like (stable) and state-like (changeable) biomarkers in psychiatric disorders, with a focus on developing computational methods to differentiate between these types of neural signatures.

Research Approach:

  • Analyze longitudinal neuroimaging data to identify patterns that remain stable vs. those that fluctuate with symptom changes
  • Develop deep learning models to extract features that represent trait and state components
  • Evaluate the clinical utility of differentiating between stable traits and dynamic states for treatment planning

Tools & Software

Our lab develops open-source tools and software to facilitate research in computational psychiatry and neuroimaging. These resources are available to the scientific community to promote collaboration and advancement in the field.

DVAE-Neuro

A deep variational autoencoder framework specifically designed for neuroimaging data analysis, with specialized components for handling the unique characteristics of brain imaging datasets.

GitHub Repository

BrainConnect

A toolkit for functional and structural connectivity analysis in neuroimaging data, providing implementations of various network analysis methods tailored for brain connectivity research.

GitHub Repository

PsychML

A machine learning library focused on psychiatric applications, with specialized functions for processing clinical assessments, symptom data, and integrating with biological measures.

GitHub Repository

Collaborations & Partnerships

Our research thrives through collaborations with other institutions and research groups. Current collaborative projects include:

ABCD Study Working Group

Contributing to the analysis of developmental trajectories in the Adolescent Brain Cognitive Development study, focusing on stress exposure and mental health outcomes.

Collaborating with technology companies to develop and validate AI tools for mental health assessment and monitoring.
