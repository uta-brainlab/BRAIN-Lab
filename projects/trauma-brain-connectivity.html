Functional Brain Connectivity in Trauma

This project examines functional brain connectivity patterns in childhood versus adulthood trauma using data-driven approaches. We aim to understand how the timing of trauma exposure affects brain network organization and function, with implications for treatment and intervention strategies.

Key Research Questions

  • How do connectivity patterns differ between childhood and adult trauma?
  • What are the developmental impacts of early trauma on brain networks?
  • Can connectivity patterns predict treatment response?
  • How do these patterns relate to symptom presentation?

Methodology

We utilize advanced neuroimaging techniques and data-driven analysis methods to examine functional connectivity patterns. This includes resting-state fMRI, network analysis, and machine learning approaches to identify distinct patterns associated with different trauma timepoints.

Current Status

Our analyses have revealed distinct connectivity patterns associated with childhood versus adult trauma exposure. These findings are helping to inform age-specific treatment approaches and intervention strategies for trauma-related disorders.
