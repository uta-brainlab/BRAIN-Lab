Reverse Engineering for Robust MDD Biomarkers

This project aims to identify novel neural biotypes for Major Depressive Disorder (MDD) using advanced computational approaches and digital phenotyping. By leveraging machine learning and neuroimaging data, we seek to develop more precise diagnostic and treatment approaches for depression.

Key Research Questions

  • What are the distinct neural biotypes within MDD?
  • How can digital phenotyping enhance our understanding of depression subtypes?
  • What computational approaches best identify robust biomarkers?
  • How can these biomarkers inform personalized treatment strategies?

Methodology

Our approach combines neuroimaging data analysis with digital phenotyping and machine learning techniques to identify distinct neural patterns and behavioral markers associated with different MDD subtypes.

Current Status

We are currently analyzing large-scale neuroimaging datasets and developing computational models to identify robust biomarkers that can reliably distinguish between different MDD subtypes.
