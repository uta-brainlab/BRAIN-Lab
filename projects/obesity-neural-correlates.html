Neural Correlates of Obesity in Adolescents

This systematic review investigates the neural correlates of obesity in adolescent populations. By synthesizing existing research and analyzing neuroimaging findings, we aim to better understand the brain mechanisms associated with obesity development during this critical developmental period.

Key Research Questions

  • What brain regions and networks are consistently associated with adolescent obesity?
  • How do neural patterns in obese adolescents differ from healthy weight peers?
  • What is the relationship between brain development and obesity progression?
  • How can understanding neural correlates inform intervention strategies?

Methodology

Our systematic review follows PRISMA guidelines, incorporating neuroimaging studies that examine brain structure and function in adolescents with obesity. We employ meta-analytic techniques to identify consistent patterns across studies.

Current Status

The review has identified several key brain regions and networks implicated in adolescent obesity, particularly in areas related to reward processing, executive function, and appetite regulation. These findings have important implications for early intervention and treatment approaches.
