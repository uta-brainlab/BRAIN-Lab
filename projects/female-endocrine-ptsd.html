Female Endocrine Modulation in PTSD

This research investigates the complex relationship between female endocrine function and PTSD symptoms, focusing on how hormonal fluctuations may influence symptom severity, treatment response, and recovery trajectories in women with PTSD.

Key Research Questions

  • How do hormonal cycles influence PTSD symptom expression?
  • What role do reproductive hormones play in fear learning and extinction?
  • How does endocrine function affect treatment outcomes?
  • Can hormone-informed treatment timing improve efficacy?

Methodology

Our research combines endocrine assessments with neuroimaging and clinical measures to track the relationship between hormonal fluctuations and PTSD symptoms. We employ longitudinal monitoring and advanced statistical methods to identify patterns and relationships.

Current Status

Initial findings suggest significant interactions between endocrine function and PTSD symptomatology. We are currently investigating how these relationships might inform the timing and type of interventions for female patients with PTSD.
