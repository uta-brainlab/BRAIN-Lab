Predicting Treatment Response to GCMRT in Social Anxiety Disorder

This research project focuses on developing predictive models for treatment response to Guided Cognitive-Motivational Response Training (GCMRT) in individuals with Social Anxiety Disorder (SAD). By leveraging advanced computational methods and neuroimaging data, we aim to identify reliable biomarkers that can predict treatment outcomes and help optimize therapeutic interventions.

Key Research Questions

  • What neural and behavioral markers predict GCMRT treatment response?
  • How can machine learning models improve personalized treatment outcomes?
  • What are the mechanisms of action in GCMRT for social anxiety?
  • How can biomarkers inform treatment optimization?

Methodology

Our approach combines functional MRI data, clinical assessments, and machine learning to track treatment outcomes longitudinally and identify predictive patterns in neural activation and connectivity.

Current Status

Preliminary results show distinct neural activation patterns associated with treatment response, particularly in frontolimbic connectivity, and individual differences in treatment trajectory based on baseline characteristics.
