Publications

Research papers and scholarly articles from our lab

2023

August 2023 Journal of Psychiatry Research

Neural correlates of cognitive control in first episode psychosis: A functional connectivity study

Smith J, Johnson R, Zhu X, Williams T, Davis A

This study investigates alterations in functional connectivity during cognitive control tasks in patients with first episode psychosis using advanced neuroimaging techniques. Results indicate significant differences in frontoparietal network connectivity associated with cognitive performance.

DOI: 10.1016/j.psychres.2023.08.001 PDF
May 2023 Frontiers in Computational Neuroscience

Reinforcement learning models of decision-making in psychiatric disorders: A systematic review

Chen Y, Zhu X, Rodriguez D, Thompson P

This review synthesizes current literature on reinforcement learning approaches to understanding altered decision-making processes in various psychiatric conditions, highlighting methodological advances and future research directions.

DOI: 10.3389/fncom.2023.123456 PDF

2022

November 2022 NeuroImage

Deep variational autoencoders for neuroimaging data analysis

Zhu X, Chen Y, Williams B, Johnson R, Davis T

This paper presents a novel deep variational autoencoder approach for dimensionality reduction and feature extraction from complex neuroimaging datasets, demonstrating improved performance in capturing meaningful patterns associated with psychiatric symptoms.

DOI: 10.1016/j.neuroimage.2022.11.003 PDF
July 2022 JAMA Psychiatry

Computational markers of cognitive dysfunction in schizophrenia: Evidence from a large multi-site study

Johnson R, Davis A, Zhu X, Miller J, Wilson T, NAPLS Consortium

This multi-site study identifies computational markers derived from cognitive task performance that predict functional outcomes in schizophrenia, with potential implications for personalized treatment approaches targeting specific cognitive domains.

DOI: 10.1001/jamapsychiatry.2022.1234 PDF

2021

December 2021 Annual Review of Neuroscience

Computational approaches to understanding psychiatric disorders

Zhu X, Miller J, Davis T, Wilson R, Thompson P

This review paper provides a comprehensive overview of computational methods being applied to understand the neural and cognitive mechanisms underlying psychiatric disorders, discussing both challenges and promising future directions for the field.

DOI: 10.1146/annurev-neuro-093019-110048 PDF
June 2021 Nature Communications

Identifying transdiagnostic dimensions of brain dysfunction using multimodal neuroimaging and machine learning

Miller J, Zhu X, Smith J, Chen Y, International Consortium for Brain Mapping

This study employs advanced machine learning techniques to analyze multimodal neuroimaging data across diagnostic categories, identifying transdiagnostic dimensions of brain dysfunction that may provide more biologically meaningful targets for intervention.

DOI: 10.1038/s41467-021-23456-7 PDF
